    Welcome

    NumberDB is a collaborative database of numbers. The underlying philosophy is:
    Numbers in practise are determined by their first 10 digits.
    NumberDB attempts to be a tool to identify numbers, and to show known descriptions. Which numbers are stored depends on their simplicity and their relevance.
    NumberDB stores numbers in collections. Collections are families of numbers together with a description. Thus a number can appear in several collections: For example, the number 1 is in the collection of Integers, Values of the Gamma function, and it has its own collection One as it is a special number.
    Collections of numbers also contain a definition, a description of parameters, and possibly formulas, comments, references, links, programs, and further more specific information.
    Furthermore collections are given tags, which helps searchability and classification.

    Work in progress

    The database just started and is in active development. There are various development goals:
    • More numbers and collections
      Our aim is to have around 10000 collections of around 1000 numbers. This makes around 107 numbers in total, which can be reasonably searched using 10 digits. Which numbers we choose will be determined by their simplicity and significance. Contributions are greatly welcome.
    • More efficient and ergonomic search
      Search results should be ordered by significance. Moreover, registered users should be allowed to do bulk searches.
    • Giving credits
      Contributors will be named at the collection webpages.
    • New number types
      A short term goal is to include complex numbers; especially when they are more significant than their real and imaginary parts alone. A long term goal is to include p-adic numbers.
    • API and collection download
      For certain research purposes it will be useful to access the data in a programmable bulk way. With some effort, this can already be done by cloning the numberdb-data repository. It is however desirably to have a simpler interface that makes the data available user's laptops, and to open source computer algebra systems such as SageMath and Pari/GP.
    • User interaction
      Users should be able to give comments, discuss collections, declare numbers as "wanted", rate collections, have a public(?) profile which includes a list of contributions.
